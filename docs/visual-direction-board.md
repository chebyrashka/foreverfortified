# Forever Fortified Visual Direction Board

## Creative Position

Forever Fortified should feel like a premium local home improvement company: durable, direct, polished, and reassuring. The design should avoid the generic contractor-site look while still feeling grounded and easy to trust.

The brand idea is "fortified living": homes that look better, perform better, and feel safer because the work was done carefully.

## Design Personality

Keywords:
- Sturdy
- Refined
- Practical
- Protective
- Local
- Warm
- Capable

Avoid:
- Corporate franchise polish
- Cheap lead-gen landing page energy
- Overly rustic construction styling
- Decorative hard-hat/tool imagery
- Generic smiling-family stock photography
- Dark, blurry, indistinct hero images

## Primary Visual Direction: Fortified Modern

This is the recommended direction.

The site should combine strong architectural photography, confident typography, warm neutral backgrounds, and restrained accent color. It should look modern without feeling cold.

### Visual References to Pursue

Look for imagery and layout inspiration from:
- Modern residential architecture sites
- Premium roofing and exterior renovation brands
- Editorial home design magazines
- Building material manufacturers
- High-end bathroom remodel portfolios

The goal is not luxury for luxury's sake. It is trust through craft, detail, and clarity.

## Color Palette

Use a grounded, high-contrast palette with a warm accent.

### Core Colors

- Iron Charcoal: `#1F2421`
- Warm White: `#F7F3EC`
- Soft Stone: `#D8D2C7`
- Graphite: `#363A36`
- Deep Forest: `#2F4A3D`
- Copper Accent: `#B86A3D`

### Usage

- Use Warm White as the main page background.
- Use Iron Charcoal for primary text and high-contrast bands.
- Use Deep Forest for trust, stability, and brand moments.
- Use Copper Accent sparingly for CTAs, active states, and important highlights.
- Use Soft Stone for dividers, quiet backgrounds, and form fields.

### What This Should Feel Like

The palette should feel like exterior materials: steel, stone, wood, shingles, siding, warm indoor light. It should not feel like a tech startup or a generic blue contractor template.

## Typography

Recommended direction:

- Headlines: `Sora`, `Manrope`, or `Source Sans 3`
- Body: `Inter`, `Atkinson Hyperlegible`, or `Source Sans 3`
- Optional editorial accent: `Newsreader` for testimonial quotes only

Use a sturdy sans-serif headline style. Avoid overly elegant serif headlines because the brand needs durability first. If an editorial serif is used, keep it limited to testimonials or article pull quotes.

### Type Feel

Headlines should be compact, confident, and plainspoken:

- "Built to hold up."
- "Roofing that protects what matters."
- "Siding that changes how your home feels from the curb."
- "Bathroom remodels made practical, polished, and personal."

Avoid:
- "Transform your dream home today"
- "Quality you can trust"
- "Your premier remodeling partner"

Those lines are too generic.

## Photography Direction

Photography is the biggest design upgrade available.

### Hero Imagery

Preferred:
- Finished home exterior in clear daylight
- Strong roofline or siding detail
- A polished bathroom remodel with natural light
- Craftsman-level close-up of materials

Avoid:
- Stock photos where people point at blueprints
- Darkened hero images that hide the work
- Overly wide house photos with no focal point
- Generic construction workers unrelated to the company

### Image Cropping

- Use wide, cinematic hero crops.
- Use close detail crops for material confidence.
- Show before/after images at consistent aspect ratios.
- Keep faces secondary unless there are authentic team photos.

### Image Types Needed

- Home exterior hero
- Roofing detail
- Siding detail
- Bathroom remodel hero
- Crew or owner photo
- Before/after pairs
- Project gallery images
- Article thumbnails

## Layout System

Use larger, calmer sections instead of many small boxed cards.

### Section Rhythm

- Hero: full-bleed, high-impact
- Trust strip: compact and scannable
- Services: three strong visual pathways
- Feature sections: image/text pairings with generous spacing
- Testimonials: prominent, warm, and readable
- Process: simple horizontal or vertical steps
- Articles: clean editorial grid
- CTA: dark band with strong copy and a short form/button

### Cards

Use cards for individual repeated items only:
- Service cards
- Testimonials
- Articles
- Project entries

Do not place entire page sections inside cards. The site will feel more premium if sections breathe.

## UI Details

### Buttons

Primary button:
- Copper background
- Charcoal or warm white text depending on contrast
- Squared radius around 6px
- Strong hover state

Secondary button:
- Transparent or warm-white fill
- Charcoal border
- Simple arrow icon

CTA labels:
- "Request a Free Estimate"
- "Explore Roofing"
- "View Recent Work"
- "Read Homeowner Guides"

### Forms

Estimate forms should feel short and easy.

Fields:
- Name
- Email
- Phone
- Project type
- ZIP or city
- Message

Keep long forms off the homepage. The homepage can use a short CTA and send users to a dedicated estimate page.

### Icons

Use restrained line icons only when helpful:
- Roofline
- Siding panel
- Bath/shower
- Shield/check
- Calendar
- Arrow

Avoid cartoon construction icons.

## Homepage Mood

The homepage should move like this:

1. Confidence
2. Clarity
3. Proof
4. Craft
5. Ease
6. Action

The user should understand within five seconds:

- What the company does
- Why it feels trustworthy
- What they should click next

## Service Page Mood

### Roofing

Mood:
- Protective
- Weather-ready
- Practical

Visual cues:
- Rooflines
- Shingles
- Flashing/detail shots
- Storm-resistant language if accurate

Sample headline:
"Roofing built for weather, wear, and peace of mind."

### Siding

Mood:
- Exterior transformation
- Curb appeal
- Lower maintenance

Visual cues:
- Crisp siding lines
- Corner trim
- Before/after exterior shots
- Material/color selections

Sample headline:
"Siding that strengthens your home from the outside in."

### Bathroom Remodel

Mood:
- Warm
- Personal
- Functional
- Polished

Visual cues:
- Tile
- Fixtures
- Vanities
- Natural light
- Storage details

Sample headline:
"Bathroom remodels that make everyday routines feel better."

## Testimonial Style

Testimonials should feel like real homeowner proof.

Preferred format:

- Quote
- Name or initials
- City
- Project type
- Optional rating

Example layout:

> "They communicated clearly, kept the work area clean, and the finished siding made the whole house feel new."

Customer Name, Siding Project, City

Use a warmer background and slightly larger quote typography so this section feels human.

## Article / Blog Style

The articles section should feel like practical homeowner guidance, not company news.

Categories:
- Roofing
- Siding
- Bathroom Remodels
- Home Maintenance
- Planning and Budgeting

Article cards:
- Image
- Category
- Title
- Short excerpt
- Read time

Tone:
- Helpful
- Direct
- Plainspoken
- Locally aware

## Design Risks to Avoid

- Too many dark sections, which can make the site feel heavy.
- Too much green, which can flatten the brand into one color.
- Too many service cards, badges, and boxes above the fold.
- Stock photos that look unrelated to the actual company.
- Generic copy that could belong to any contractor.
- Over-designed animations that slow down the quote path.

## Implementation Notes for Astro and Storyblok

Build the visual system as reusable components:

- `HeroSection`
- `TrustStrip`
- `ServiceCardGrid`
- `FeatureSplit`
- `ProjectSpotlight`
- `BeforeAfter`
- `TestimonialGrid`
- `ProcessSteps`
- `ArticleGrid`
- `CtaBand`
- `EstimateForm`

In Storyblok, editors should choose from these blocks rather than free-designing pages. This gives them publishing freedom while keeping the brand visually consistent.

## Recommended First Design Pass

Start with these screens:

- Homepage
- Roofing service page
- Bathroom remodel service page
- Article listing page
- Article detail page
- Contact / estimate page

Once those are strong, siding and project pages can follow the same system.
